Hilton Tokyo Bay is a Tokyo Disney Resort Official Hotel with 828 rooms, located directly across from Bayside Station. It stands out for its **24-hour in-house convenience store** — a rarity among Official Hotels — along with fairy-tale-themed rooms and indoor/outdoor pools that make it a favorite among families. This review covers the hotel's strengths, weaknesses, and access from a local perspective. Prices and times are estimates based on 2025–2026 research.

## Best For

- **Guests who want peace of mind for late-night shopping** — The in-house 24-hour convenience store is unique among Official Hotels in the Urayasu area
- **Families with young children** — Family Happy Magic rooms sleep 2–6 guests, with children's amenities included
- **Airport arrivals** — Dedicated airport limousine stop right at the hotel entrance for Haneda and Narita
- **Extended stays** — 24-hour coin laundry in B1

## May Not Suit

- **Guests who want to walk directly to the parks** — The hotel is adjacent to the parks, but the Disney Resort Line monorail is the standard way to get there
- **Guests who want to walk from Maihama Station** — Distance makes a shuttle or taxi more practical

## Access

### To Tokyo Disneyland (TDL)
Free shuttle + Disney Resort Line to Tokyo Disneyland Station, approx. 10 min.

### To Tokyo DisneySea (TDS)
Free shuttle + Disney Resort Line to Tokyo DisneySea Station, approx. 10 min.

### To JR Station
A free shuttle bus runs to Maihama Station in about 7 minutes. The stop is at the rotary to the right of Maihama Station South Exit. Walking is not practical; the shuttle or a taxi is the realistic option.

### To the Airport
**The hotel has a dedicated airport limousine bus stop** at the entrance. Haneda Airport: approx. 60 min, ¥1,300. Narita Airport: approx. 90 min, ¥1,300. Boarding and alighting at the hotel entrance — convenient even with heavy luggage.
> Note: Advance reservation required for the limousine bus (reserved seating).

### To Central Tokyo
From Maihama Station, about 15–20 minutes to Tokyo Station on the JR Keiyo Line.

## Facilities & Services

{{< hotel-facilities >}}

**In-House Convenience Store (24-Hour — the biggest practical advantage)**
"Seikatsu Saika" on the 1st floor is open 24 hours for drinks, snacks, and daily goods (pharmaceuticals sold 8:00–22:00). "S-Lawson" is also on the same floor (17:00–23:00). **The only 24-hour in-house convenience store among Official Hotels in the Urayasu area** — guests from neighboring Okura and Sheraton come here to shop, a well-known fact locally.

**Guest Rooms**
828 rooms with a wide variety of types. Beyond the Hilton Room (40 m²), Family Happy Magic Rooms (35–40 m², 2 beds + bunk) accommodate 2–6 guests. Bed guards, baby cots, and children's amenities are available at no extra charge.

**Baths & Pool**
No natural hot spring, but B1 has bath facilities and a pool (fee: ¥4,000; free for Hilton Honors Gold and above). Indoor and outdoor pools, and a 24-hour free fitness center.

**Breakfast**
Japanese, Western, and Chinese buffet with a chef-made omelet station and a Kids Breakfast section popular with children.

**Coin Laundry**
24-hour coin laundry in B1 (fee applies). Vending machines, free Wi-Fi, and TV are in the laundry room. Paid laundry service also available.

**Parking**
¥3,100 for 1 night / ¥4,700 for 2 nights / ¥6,300 for 3+ nights (tax included). Available from 15:00 on check-in day to 30 minutes after check-out. Some Family Happy Magic room plans include free parking (except Dec. 31).

**Luggage**
Cloakroom at the lobby service counter. Baggage delivery from Maihama Station Welcome Center to the hotel, and home delivery (paid) are available.

**Wi-Fi & Multilingual**
Free Wi-Fi throughout. Multilingual staff available.

## Local Notes

The biggest practical advantage of Hilton Tokyo Bay is undoubtedly the **24-hour in-house convenience store**. While Official Hotels in the Maihama area compete on roughly equal terms, the confidence that comes from knowing you can shop at midnight is a significant factor for families. Guests from neighboring hotels are a common sight here — that's how trusted this store is locally.

Peak times for the front desk are 15:00–17:30, about 1 hour after park closing, and 11:00–12:00. The hotel officially recommends avoiding these windows for a smoother check-in or check-out experience.
