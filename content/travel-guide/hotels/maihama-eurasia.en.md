Maihama Eurasia is uniquely positioned as the **only hotel in the Maihama resort zone with a natural hot spring open to the public**. Outdoor bath, cave bath, sauna, stone bath, and foot bath are all available — a comprehensive hot spring facility. Shuttle buses run from three stations: Maihama, Urayasu, and Shin-Urayasu. Day-trip hot spring visits (without an overnight stay) are also accepted. The nearest convenience store is 10 minutes on foot, and check-out is 10:00 — earlier than most area hotels. This review covers strengths, weaknesses, and access. Prices and times are estimates based on 2025–2026 research.

## Access

### To the Parks (TDL / TDS)
Hotel shuttle to Maihama Station area. From Maihama Station, Disney Resort Cruiser or Disney Resort Line to the parks.

### To JR Station
Shuttle from Maihama Station (approx. 10–15 min). Also from Urayasu Station and Shin-Urayasu Station (shuttle route serves all three).

### To the Airport
From Maihama Station bus stop, airport limousine to Haneda: approx. 40 min, ¥1,300. Narita: approx. 75 min, ¥2,900.

### To Central Tokyo
From Maihama Station, about 15 minutes to Tokyo Station on the JR Keiyo Line.

## Local Notes

Maihama Eurasia's hot spring is the single most distinctive accommodation feature in the entire Maihama resort area. Even Hotel Emion Tokyo Bay (Partner Hotel, also a natural hot spring) is not in the Maihama resort zone proper — Eurasia is alone in this. Guests at other Maihama-area hotels who want to soak in a natural hot spring during their TDR trip most naturally end up here.

The day-trip hot spring option is a meaningful service: guests staying at Official Hotels or other area hotels can use Eurasia's hot spring as a standalone visit without booking a room. Given the proximity to the parks, this can be part of a broader itinerary.

The 10:00 check-out and 10-minute walk to the nearest convenience store are the main practical trade-offs to know before booking.
