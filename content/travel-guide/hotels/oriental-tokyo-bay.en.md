Hotel Oriental Tokyo Bay is a TDR Partner Hotel offering an exceptional shuttle frequency: **69 free shuttle buses per day** — far more than other Partner Hotels. The deck-connected 1-minute walk to Shin-Urayasu Station, a 24-hour in-house convenience store, 2F coin laundry, and Cinderella Castle-themed rooms are additional standouts. An 8.5-meter-diameter projection mapping installation in the lobby is one of the hotel's visual highlights. This review covers strengths, weaknesses, and access. Prices and times are estimates based on 2025–2026 research.

## Best For

- **Guests who want to maximize park time from opening to late closing** — 69 shuttle buses per day means you're rarely waiting more than a few minutes for the next departure
- **Guests who need a 24-hour in-house convenience store** — Available inside the hotel at all hours; only Mitsui Garden Prana offers similar among Partner Hotels
- **Families who love Cinderella and want themed rooms** — Cinderella Castle themed rooms available; unique among Partner Hotels
- **Guests who need to do laundry during multi-night stays** — 2F coin laundry available

## May Not Suit

- **Guests who want a large communal bath or natural hot spring** — No large bath or hot spring; for that, consider Emion
- **Guests wanting a quieter, smaller hotel** — 511 rooms makes Oriental one of the larger Partner Hotels; lobby and elevators can be busy at peak times

## Access

### To Tokyo Disneyland
Free shuttle bus (up to 69/day, first bus 7:00), approx. 15 min.

### To Tokyo DisneySea
Free shuttle bus (up to 69/day, first bus 7:00), approx. 15 min.

### To JR Station
**Deck connection to Shin-Urayasu Station: approx. 1 min (deck bridge)**. Same as Brighton — both are directly deck-connected to the station. Convenient for train travelers even in rain.

### To the Airport
From "Shin-Urayasu Station" bus stop (South Exit, stop H; 1–2 min from hotel via deck): Haneda approx. 40–45 min, ¥1,300. Narita approx. 60 min, ¥2,900 (Tokyo Airport Transport / Keiseibus Chiba West; advance reservation recommended during peak seasons).
> Note: No dedicated hotel stop; a short walk to Shin-Urayasu Station bus stop is required.

### To Central Tokyo
From Shin-Urayasu Station, about 20 minutes to Tokyo Station on the JR Keiyo Line.

**TDR Shuttle (69 buses/day — the area's highest frequency)**
69 free shuttle buses per day eliminates scheduling stress. Whether you're heading to park opening, returning for lunch, or staying until closing, a shuttle is always within minutes. Particularly valuable on busy days and during school holidays.

**Projection Mapping (8.5m diameter in lobby)**
A large projection mapping display in the lobby — a visual spectacle at hotel arrival and when passing through. Unusual feature for this category.

**24-Hour In-House Convenience Store**
Open 24 hours. Among the four Partner Hotels, only Oriental and Mitsui Garden Prana have in-house convenience stores, and Oriental's is confirmed 24-hour.

Cinderella Castle-themed rooms available (limited). Ceiling design and furnishings reflect the TDL icon. Book early for these room types.

## Local Notes

Hotel Oriental Tokyo Bay's identity is built on **shuttle frequency**. 69 buses per day to TDR isn't a small difference from other Partner Hotels — it fundamentally changes how freely you can move between the hotel and the parks. Returning to the hotel for lunch, going back to the park, leaving early for dinner and coming back — all of these become practical decisions rather than logistics puzzles.

Combined with the 24-hour in-house convenience store and deck connection to Shin-Urayasu Station, Oriental has assembled a remarkably practical feature set. The main trade-offs are the larger scale (511 rooms) and no large communal bath. Within the four Partner Hotels, Oriental and Brighton often compete for the top recommendation; Brighton wins on station connection convenience, Oriental wins decisively on shuttle frequency.
