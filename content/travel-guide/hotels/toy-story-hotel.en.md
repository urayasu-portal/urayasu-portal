Toy Story Hotel is a Disney Hotel opened in 2022 with a theme based on the interior of Andy's room from the Toy Story films — guests become toys in a child's room. With 595 rooms, the hotel is adjacent to Bayside Station (Disney Resort Line), and the Fantasy Springs area of TDS becomes accessible via the hotel's dedicated entrance from 1 hour after park opening (10:00 for a 9:00 opening). 3F coin laundry, in-house shop open 6:00–24:00, and a dedicated airport limousine stop at the entrance round out the hotel's facilities. This review covers strengths, weaknesses, and access. Prices and times are estimates based on 2025–2026 research.

## Best For

- **Guests who love Toy Story and want to sleep "inside Andy's room"** — The entire room is designed as if seen from a toy's perspective in Andy's room; Woody and Buzz figures and oversized furniture create genuine immersion
- **Guests who want Happy Entry at a mid-tier Disney Hotel price** — 15-minute early park admission; more affordable than Tokyo Disneyland Hotel or Fantasy Springs Hotel
- **Guests who want Fantasy Springs access from inside the hotel** — A dedicated entrance to Fantasy Springs opens from 10:00 (1 hour after park opening); combines with Happy Entry at the main gate
- **Guests arriving at Bayside Station** — Hotel is adjacent to Bayside Station; close connection to the Disney Resort Line

## May Not Suit

- **Guests who want to walk to TDL in 1 minute** — TDL requires the Disney Resort Line from Bayside Station; not walking-distance to TDL (unlike Tokyo Disneyland Hotel)
- **Guests on a tight budget** — Starting at approx. ¥45,000; not the most affordable Disney Hotel option
- **Guests who need a convenience store at the hotel** — No 24-hour in-house convenience store; in-house shop is open 6:00–24:00 but may not cover all late-night needs

## Access

### To Tokyo Disneyland
Bayside Station + Disney Resort Line to Tokyo Disneyland Station (15-min early entry, all rooms).

### To Tokyo DisneySea
Bayside Station + Disney Resort Line to Tokyo DisneySea Station.

### To JR Station
Via Disney Resort Line from Bayside Station to Resort Gateway Station, then to Maihama Station, approx. 10–15 min total.

### To the Airport
**Dedicated airport limousine stop at the hotel entrance.** Haneda: approx. 40–50 min, ¥1,300. Narita: approx. 62–67 min, ¥2,900 (advance reservation recommended during peak seasons).

### To Central Tokyo
From Maihama Station, about 15 minutes to Tokyo Station on the JR Keiyo Line.

## Facilities & Services
{{< hotel-facilities >}}

**Guest Rooms (595 rooms, Andy's room theme)**
All rooms designed from the perspective of toys in Andy's bedroom — oversized furniture, crayon-colored walls, Woody/Buzz/Rex and other Toy Story characters in the decor. A distinctive immersion that no other Disney Hotel replicates. Room types include standard (up to 4 guests), deluxe, and suites.

**Happy Entry**
15-minute early park admission to TDL.

**Fantasy Springs Hotel Entrance (from 10:00)**
A dedicated entrance to the Fantasy Springs expansion area is accessible from the hotel from 1 hour after park opening (10:00 for a 9:00 opening). Note: this is NOT a separate attraction ticket — standard TDS park admission is required.

**In-House Shop**
Open 6:00–24:00. Good range of hours for early departures and late-night needs (light meals, snacks, souvenirs). Not a 24-hour convenience store.

**3F Coin Laundry**
Available on 3F (fee applies).

**Breakfast**
Available from 6:30am (confirm details on official website).

**Parking**
¥3,000 for 1st night; ¥1,000 per additional night.

**Wi-Fi**
Free Wi-Fi in all rooms.

## Local Notes

Toy Story Hotel's identity is clear: **the room theme is the reason to stay here.** If the Toy Story concept resonates with you or your family — particularly if you have children who know and love these characters — the immersive quality of waking up as a toy in Andy's room is hard to match. It's a younger, more playful Disney Hotel than the Victorian style of Tokyo Disneyland Hotel.

The Fantasy Springs hotel entrance (from 10:00) is a bonus for TDS-focused visits, though it requires separate park admission and opens after the first hour — not for rushing to attractions at opening. For opening rush, use the main gate with Happy Entry.
