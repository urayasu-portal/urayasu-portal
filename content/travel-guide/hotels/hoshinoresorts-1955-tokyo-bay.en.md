Hoshino Resorts BEB5 Tokyo Bay opened in June 2024 as Hoshino Resorts' first property in Chiba prefecture. The hotel's 1955 America vintage diner theme runs throughout the 638 rooms (23 types) and public spaces. An in-house Lawson is available, and 339 parking spaces are offered at just ¥500 per 12 hours — the lowest parking rate among hotels near TDR. The "Bus Number 1955" shuttle runs to TDR in approximately 30 minutes. No large communal bath. This review covers the hotel's strengths, weaknesses, and access. Prices and times are estimates based on 2025–2026 research.

## Local Notes

Hoshino Resorts BEB5 Tokyo Bay's headline practical feature is the parking rate: **¥500 per 12 hours**. If you're staying 2 nights and driving, you'll pay ¥500×4 = ¥2,000 total in parking — versus ¥4,000–¥6,000 at most area hotels. For families who drive and stay 2–3 nights, the parking savings can offset a meaningful portion of the nightly room rate.

The Hoshino Resorts brand brings a design and hospitality sensibility that clearly differs from generic business hotels. BEB5 is their "casual" line — targeted at younger travelers and families who want the brand feel without the OMO or Kai pricing. June 2024 opening means the facilities are still essentially new.

The 30-minute shuttle to TDR and no large bath are the main trade-offs. For guests who prioritize parking value and fresh design over park proximity, BEB5 is one of the stronger options in the ¥18,000–¥50,000 range.
