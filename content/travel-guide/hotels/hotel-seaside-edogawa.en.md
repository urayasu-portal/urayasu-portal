Hotel Seaside Edogawa is an urban resort set inside the vast Kasai Rinkai Park. With 29 rooms in total, it offers 14 Japanese-style rooms — rare for the bay area — making it easy to use for families and groups. It has separate large baths for men and women and is about a 3-minute walk from JR Keiyo Line Kasai-Rinkai-Koen Station, just one stop from Maihama (Tokyo Disney Resort). Prices and times are estimates based on 2025–2026 research.

## Best For

- **Families and groups who want a Japanese-style room** — 14 Japanese-style rooms, rare for the bay area
- **Guests who want a large bath** — Separate large baths for men and women
- **Guests who enjoy a park-and-sea setting** — Urban resort inside Kasai Rinkai Park, ~3 min from the station

## May Not Suit

- **Guests who shop late at night** — Park location; confirm nearby convenience options in advance
- **Guests who want many room types to choose from** — A small hotel with 29 rooms

## Access

### To Tokyo Disneyland (TDL)
One stop on the JR Keiyo Line to Maihama, then Disney Resort Line to Tokyo Disneyland Station.

### To Tokyo DisneySea (TDS)
One stop on the JR Keiyo Line to Maihama, then Disney Resort Line to Tokyo DisneySea Station.

### To the Nearest Station
About 3 min on foot from JR Keiyo Line Kasai-Rinkai-Koen Station.

### To the Airport
About 60 min to Haneda Airport via Tokyo Station.

### To Central Tokyo
About 15 min from Kasai-Rinkai-Koen Station to Tokyo Station on the JR Keiyo Line.

## Local Notes

Surrounded by the greenery and sea breeze of Kasai Rinkai Park, this is one of the few bayside hotels offering both Japanese-style rooms and a large bath. Being one stop from Maihama on the Keiyo Line is a real convenience.
