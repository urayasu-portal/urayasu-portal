Hyatt Regency Tokyo Bay is a Shin-Urayasu hotel from the Hyatt brand, carrying the "Regency" tier designation. Since **December 20, 2024, a dedicated airport limousine bus stop was added directly at the hotel entrance** — previously, guests had to walk to the Shin-Urayasu Station stop. This addition significantly upgraded the hotel's airport access. All rooms feature a separated wash area and bathroom. Children 12 and under sleep free. Chromecast is installed in all rooms for streaming your own content. This review covers the hotel's strengths, weaknesses, and access. Prices and times are estimates based on 2025–2026 research.

## Best For

- **Guests arriving or departing by airport limousine** — Dedicated limousine stop added December 2024 at the hotel entrance; Haneda and Narita both served with zero-minute walk to the bus stop
- **Guests who want a club lounge experience** — Hyatt Regency Club floor provides lounge access, upgraded amenities, and complimentary food and drinks throughout the day
- **Families with children under 12** — Children 12 and under sleep free (same room, sharing existing beds)
- **Guests who want to stream their own content in the room** — Chromecast built in; connect your phone or tablet to the room TV via Chromecast

## May Not Suit

- **Guests who prioritize being closest to the parks** — Free shuttle to TDR takes approx. 20 min; location is Shin-Urayasu, not Maihama
- **Guests wanting a natural hot spring** — No natural hot spring (for a Partner Hotel with hot spring, consider Emion)
- **Budget-conscious guests** — Standard rates from approx. ¥25,000–¥60,000; the Regency tier has a corresponding price level

## Access

### To Tokyo Disneyland (TDL)
Free shuttle bus (not eligible for TDR official benefits), approx. 20 min.

### To Tokyo DisneySea (TDS)
Free shuttle bus (not eligible for TDR official benefits), approx. 20 min.

### To JR Station
About 10 min on foot to Shin-Urayasu Station. Also accessible by hotel shuttle (confirm schedule). Covered walkway access to the Shin-Urayasu Station area via the deck (confirm exact route at front desk).

### To the Airport
**Dedicated airport limousine stop at the hotel entrance (added December 20, 2024).** Haneda: approx. 40–45 min, ¥1,300. Narita: approx. 60 min, ¥2,900 (Tokyo Airport Transport / Keiseibus Chiba West; advance reservation recommended during peak seasons).

### To Central Tokyo
From Shin-Urayasu Station, about 20 minutes to Tokyo Station on the JR Keiyo Line.

## Local Notes

The December 2024 dedicated limousine stop addition is a meaningful practical upgrade for Hyatt Regency. Previously, guests had to walk to Shin-Urayasu Station's bus stop — now the limousine boards and alights at the hotel entrance itself, making airport connections as smooth as an Official Hotel's limousine experience.

Chromecast in all rooms is appreciated by families who want to put on familiar content for children before bed, or guests who want to stream their own films — a detail that matters more in practice than it sounds on paper.

For a Hyatt Regency stay near TDR that isn't a Disney Hotel but provides upscale comfort, dedicated limousine access, and club lounge options, this hotel has a clear niche.
