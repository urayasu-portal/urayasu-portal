Urayasu Brighton Hotel Tokyo Bay is a Tokyo Disney Resort Partner Hotel connected to Shin-Urayasu Station by a covered deck just 80 meters away — the closest station-to-hotel connection among the four Partner Hotels. With up to 34 free shuttle buses per day running from park opening to after closing, and a convenience store steps away, it's a highly functional hotel for train travelers and families managing large amounts of luggage. This review covers the hotel's strengths, weaknesses, and access from a local perspective. Prices and times are estimates based on 2025–2026 research.

## Best For

- **Guests who prefer trains and guests with heavy luggage** — Covered deck connection to Shin-Urayasu Station (approx. 80m); no umbrella needed even in rain
- **Guests who want to make the most of the parks from opening to closing** — Up to 34 free shuttle buses per day, running from before opening to after closing
- **Guests who need a late-night convenience store** — Lawson steps from the hotel, open 24 hours
- **Guests wanting generous room space** — Core rooms at 42 m², plus unique types like the View Bath and the "Room Danran" (a floor-sitting relaxation style)
- **Families and groups of up to 5** — Room types available for up to 5 guests (Room Forest, etc.)

## May Not Suit

- **Guests wanting a large communal bath or hot spring** — No large bath or hot spring; for a natural hot spring among Partner Hotels, consider Hotel Emion Tokyo Bay
- **Guests wanting a coin laundry** — No in-house coin laundry (nearby facilities can be recommended by the hotel)
- **Guests arriving directly from the airport** — No dedicated limousine stop at the hotel; must walk 1–2 min to Shin-Urayasu Station bus stop
- **Groups of 6 or more adults** — Rooms primarily accommodate up to 5

## Access

### To Tokyo Disneyland (TDL)
Free Partner Hotel shuttle (up to 34/day), approx. 15 min.

### To Tokyo DisneySea (TDS)
Free Partner Hotel shuttle (up to 34/day), approx. 15 min.

### To JR Station
**Covered deck connection to Shin-Urayasu Station: approx. 1 min (approx. 80m)**. The closest station connection among all four Partner Hotels; no umbrella needed even in heavy rain. Very practical for guests with large suitcases.

### To the Airport
Haneda: approx. 40–45 min, ¥1,300 from "Shin-Urayasu Station" bus stop (1–2 min walk from hotel, South Exit stop H). Narita: approx. 60 min from the same stop (Tokyo Airport Transport / Keiseibus Chiba West; advance reservation recommended during peak seasons).
> Note: No dedicated hotel stop; a short walk to Shin-Urayasu Station bus stop is required.

### To Central Tokyo
From Shin-Urayasu Station, about 17 min to Tokyo Station on the JR Keiyo Line express. Direct access to Maihama Station is also available.

## Facilities & Services

**Guest Rooms (distinctive room types)**
189 rooms across 22 floors at a core size of 42 m². Unique options include the "View Bath" (soaking tub with views of Urayasu at night) and "Room Danran" (floor-sitting style for relaxed stays). Up to 5 guests in Room Forest (42 m²). 189 rooms means less congestion in hallways and elevators.

**Breakfast**
Restaurant "Cachoucachou" on the 1F buffet (for guests on a breakfast-included plan). "Katsu-Vegetable" breakfast with Japanese options.

**Convenience Store (24-hour)**
Lawson steps from the hotel, open 24 hours. Handles post-park snacks and late-night needs without any hassle.

**Parking**
¥3,000 per night per vehicle (details may vary by plan; confirm at booking).

**Luggage**
Same-day luggage storage for early arrivals. Baggage delivery service (paid, ¥800/item) from the park to the hotel also available for hands-free park visit after check-out.

**Wi-Fi**
Free Wi-Fi in all rooms.

## Local Notes

Among the four Shin-Urayasu Partner Hotels (Brighton, Oriental, Emion, Mitsui Garden), **Brighton wins outright for "station access."** The covered deck connection to Shin-Urayasu Station in 1 minute / 80 meters makes a real difference in rain, when you have heavy luggage, or when you're exhausted. The deck also connects to MONA Shin-Urayasu (Aeon Style) and Atre Shin-Urayasu (currently under renovation — check operating status), so dinner and shopping beyond Disney are manageable.

The clear trade-offs are no large communal bath (versus Emion's natural hot spring or Mitsui Garden's panoramic bath), and no in-house coin laundry. If multi-night stays requiring laundry are planned, check nearby facilities in advance or consider another hotel. Brighton is at its best for guests who want to prioritize station access and mobility above all else.
