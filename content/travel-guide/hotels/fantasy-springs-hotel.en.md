Fantasy Springs Hotel, which opened in June 2024 alongside the Fantasy Springs expansion at Tokyo DisneySea, is the newest Disney Hotel at Tokyo Disney Resort. It consists of two buildings: **Grand Chateau** (56 rooms, direct access into Fantasy Springs with 8 attraction tickets included, ultra-luxury pricing) and **Fantasy Chateau** (419 rooms, deluxe, standard Disney Hotel benefits). Happy Entry early admission applies to both. This review covers the hotel's strengths, weaknesses, and access from a local perspective. Prices and times are estimates based on 2025–2026 research.

## Best For

- **Guests who want to be the first into Fantasy Springs at the highest level of access** — Grand Chateau guests have direct access into Fantasy Springs plus 8 attraction tickets (included in room rate); the most privileged Fantasy Springs experience available
- **Guests wanting to stay in the newest Disney Hotel (2024)** — Fantasy Springs Hotel as a whole is the most recently opened Disney Hotel at TDS, with brand-new interiors and facilities
- **Guests who want Happy Entry early park admission** — 15-minute early admission to the parks applies to both buildings (confirm on official website for current scope)
- **Couples and guests seeking a luxurious Disney Hotel experience** — Fantasy Chateau is a high-end deluxe Disney Hotel on par with Grand Nikko

## May Not Suit

- **Guests on a budget** — Fantasy Chateau starts at approx. ¥50,000–¥100,000+; Grand Chateau can reach several hundred thousand yen per night
- **Guests who need easy access without navigating TDS** — The hotel is inside TDS; check-in and check-out involve passing through the park (standard Disney Hotel check-in flow)
- **Guests primarily visiting TDL** — Access to TDL requires the Disney Resort Line (paid monorail)

## Access

### To Tokyo Disneyland (TDL)
Via the Disney Resort Line (15-min early entry, all rooms).

### To Tokyo DisneySea (TDS)
Direct from the private 'Grand Chateau Gateway' entrance (Grand guests); 15-min early entry.

### To JR Station
To Maihama Station: the Disney Resort Line (paid) to Resort Gateway Station. No practical walking route.

### To the Airport
**Dedicated airport limousine bus stop at the hotel.** Haneda: approx. 40–50 min, ¥1,300. Narita: approx. 75 min, ¥2,900 (advance reservation recommended during peak seasons).

### To Central Tokyo
From Maihama Station, about 15 minutes to Tokyo Station on the JR Keiyo Line.

## Facilities & Services

**Grand Chateau (56 rooms — the most exclusive option)**
- Direct access passageway into the Fantasy Springs area (accessible at specific hours; confirm on official website)
- 8 attraction tickets (for Fantasy Springs attractions) included per room stay
- Ultra-luxury specifications; the highest-tier Disney Hotel in Japan
- Room rate: from several hundred thousand yen per night (booking opens primarily by lottery)

**Fantasy Chateau (419 rooms — deluxe Disney Hotel standard)**
- Room rate: approx. ¥50,000–¥100,000+ for 2 guests in standard period
- All rooms themed around the four Fantasy Springs stories (Frozen, Rapunzel, Peter Pan, Tinker Bell)
- Breakfast available (details: confirm on official website)

**Happy Entry**
15-minute early park admission applies to both buildings. Grand Chateau guests also have the direct access benefit (confirm full scope on official website).

**Pools / Baths**
Large pool and relaxation facilities (confirm current operating status and fees on official website).

**Coin Laundry / Convenience Store**
In-house convenience store and coin laundry (confirm on official website).

**Parking**
¥3,000 for 1st night; ¥1,000 per additional night.

**Wi-Fi**
Free Wi-Fi in rooms.

## Local Notes

Fantasy Springs Hotel is clearly the most exciting new Disney Hotel in years. **Grand Chateau's direct access to Fantasy Springs + 8 attraction tickets** is unlike anything previously offered at TDR. For guests who want to experience the new Fantasy Springs area at the absolute highest level, this is the only way to get it — but booking is fiercely competitive (lottery-based) and pricing reflects the demand.

Fantasy Chateau is more accessible but still a premium Disney Hotel on par with Grand Nikko or Hotel Okura Tokyo Bay in pricing. If you want the new, immersive Fantasy Springs rooms and Happy Entry, Fantasy Chateau delivers that at more consistent availability than Grand Chateau.

Both buildings are dramatically different experiences. Be clear about which one you're targeting when booking.

Fantasy Chateau: approx. ¥50,000–¥100,000+ for 2 guests. Grand Chateau: from several hundred thousand yen per night (varies by room type; dynamic pricing). Check the latest availability on the official TDR website or booking sites.
