The moment you step off the train at JR Maihama Station, you face a choice: drag your suitcases into the parks, or drop them and enjoy the day hands-free. Maihama has services that **deliver your bags from the station to your hotel**, and whether it's free or paid — and which counter you use — depends on your hotel's category. This guide walks through the **steps, locations, hours, and fees in the order you'll actually use them**. Hours and fees can change, so reconfirm on each official site (details are from our 2025–2026 research).

For the bigger picture of *which* hotels use *which* system, see our [complete guide to luggage storage & baggage delivery](/en/travel-guide/hotels/luggage/). This article focuses on *how* to actually use it on the day.

---

## First, check: which system does your hotel use?

| Your hotel | Station-to-hotel delivery | Counter |
|---|---|---|
| Disney Hotels | **Free** | Welcome Center 2F |
| Official Hotels | **Free** | Welcome Center 1F |
| Partner Hotels | **Paid — ¥800/item** | Bon Voyage 1F |
| Celebration Hotels | **Paid** (fee not published) | Bon Voyage 1F |
| Other hotels | No station delivery | — |

Staying at an "other" hotel? There's no station delivery, so use the hotel's own front-desk storage instead (see [each hotel's luggage handling](/en/travel-guide/hotels/luggage/)).

---

## Pattern A: Free Welcome Center delivery (Disney & Official)

### Step 1｜Go to the Welcome Center
Leave the JR Maihama Station **south exit and turn left** — the **Tokyo Disney Resort Welcome Center** is right there, next to the station.

### Step 2｜Find your hotel's counter
- **Disney Hotels** — 2nd-floor Disney Hotel Service Counter (**7:30–16:00**)
- **Official Hotels** — 1st-floor Hotel Service Counter (**8:00–15:00**)

> **When it gets busy**: Maihama View Hotel's official guidance notes that **8:00–9:30** is crowded for baggage drop-off and **9:00–12:00** for check-in paperwork. Arrive early or slightly off-peak to move faster.

### Step 3｜Drop your bags (free) — and do more while you're there
Hand your bags to the counter (free). **Valuables, fragile items, precision devices (laptops, cameras), drinks, and refrigerated items can't be sent**, so move those into your day bag first.

You can also handle these at the same counter:
- **Disney Hotels**: complete check-in by tapping the "Check-in" button in the official app (the 2F counter handles baggage, park-ticket sales, and online check-in reception)
- **Official Hotels**: complete pre-check-in, and — on eligible plans — buy park tickets here too (cash or credit card only)

### Step 4｜Head into the parks hands-free
Your bags are delivered to the hotel. The official sources don't state whether they arrive in your room or wait at the bell desk, nor a delivery time, so **check the pick-up details with your hotel's front desk at check-in**.

## Pattern B: Paid Bon Voyage delivery (Partner & Celebration)

### Step 1｜Go to Bon Voyage
As you leave the station ticket gates, look **ahead and to the right** for a distinctive building **shaped like a giant suitcase and hat box** — that's the Disney store "Bon Voyage." It has two floors: the **1st floor holds the service facilities** (the delivery counter), and the 2nd floor sells merchandise.

### Step 2｜Come within reception hours (open–15:00)
Reception runs on your **check-in day**, from when Bon Voyage opens (hours are usually **8:00**–22:00) until **15:00**. Even on days when Bon Voyage requires an advance entry reservation for shopping, **baggage delivery needs no reservation** — just tell the staff at the entrance you'd like to use it.

### Step 3｜Drop your bags at the 1F counter
Use the "Home Delivery Service Counter" on the store's 1st floor. The fee is **¥800 per item (tax included) for the four Partner Hotels**. Celebration Hotel guests use the same counter, but the **fee is not published** — confirm it on site. As in Pattern A, valuables, fragile items, precision devices, and drinks can't be sent.

### Step 4｜Head into the parks hands-free
Your bags are delivered to your hotel the same day. As with Pattern A, the pick-up point and arrival time aren't published, so confirm at the front desk when you check in.

## Going home: "Station Delivery" from hotel to station

Send your bags to the station after check-out and you can enjoy the final day hands-free too. This works for **Disney and Official hotels** (not Celebration Hotels).

1. **Drop at the hotel**: after check-out, arrange it at the bell desk (**¥800 per item**; pay by cash, credit card, or some e-money). **The drop-off deadline is set by each hotel** (e.g. Sheraton takes bags at the 2F bell desk until **12:30**).
2. **Earlier is better**: at Maihama View Hotel, your drop-off time sets when pick-up opens (by 9:00 → from 13:00 / 9:00–10:30 → from 14:00 / 10:30–12:30 → from 15:30).
3. **Collect at the Welcome Center 1F counter**: pick-up hours differ — **13:00–21:00 for Disney Hotels, until 20:00 for Official hotels**.
4. **Mind the deadline**: bags left past the pick-up window are returned to the hotel (per Maihama View). Even if you're staying for the nighttime show, **collect your bags before wandering off after park close**.

## Rules that apply to all, and a backup plan

<div class="tg-notice-info">
<i class="ti ti-alert-triangle" aria-hidden="true"></i> <strong>Can't be sent:</strong> valuables (wallet, phone), fragile items, precision devices (laptops, cameras, game consoles), drinks (opened or alcoholic), and perishable or refrigerated items are excluded from every service — keep them with you. Hours and fees may change.

- **Miss the reception window, or not using delivery?** Almost every hotel holds bags at the front desk before check-in and after check-out (see [each hotel's luggage handling](/en/travel-guide/hotels/luggage/)).
- **Want to stash bags inside the park?** Use the in-park coin lockers — the station-to-hotel delivery services do not operate from inside the parks.
